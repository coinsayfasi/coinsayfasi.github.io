What to Pack for Australia

What to pack for Australia? Australia spans beaches to outback — pack sun protection, breathable layers and very good sunscreen. Here's the complete carry-on packing list — and how to keep it under your airline's cabin limit with OneBag.

👕 Clothing for Australia

  • Lightweight breathable clothes
  • Swimwear ×2
  • Sun hat (wide brim)
  • Walking shoes + thongs (flip-flops)
  • Light layer for evenings
  • Rain jacket (tropical north)

🌦️ What to wear by season

  • Remember: seasons are reversed
  • Summer (Dec–Feb): very hot, sun protection
  • Winter (Jun–Aug): mild south, warm north

🔌 Tech & power

  • Plug: Type I, 230V
  • Universal adapter
  • Power bank
  • Phone + charger
  • eSIM / local SIM
  • Headphones

🎒 Australia-specific essentials

  • Very high SPF sunscreen
  • Sunglasses & hat
  • Insect repellent
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Power bank
  • Reef-safe sunscreen (Great Barrier Reef)

📄 Documents & money

  • Passport (+ visa if required)
  • Offline copies of bookings
  • 2 payment cards (different networks)
  • Some local cash for arrival
  • Travel insurance details
  • Emergency contacts list

🧴 Toiletries (under 100ml / 3.4oz)

  • Toothbrush + mini toothpaste
  • Deodorant (solid travels best)
  • Mini shampoo / body wash
  • Sunscreen + lip balm
  • Any daily medication
  • Quick-dry travel towel
💡 OneBag tip: Long domestic flights (Jetstar, Qantas) have strict cabin limits — track your bag in OneBag.

Australia packing FAQ

Can I pack for Australia in a carry-on only?

Yes — with smart layering, one carry-on is enough for most Australia trips. Use OneBag to track your bag's weight against your exact airline limit so you never get stopped at the gate.

What plug adapter do I need for Australia?

Australia uses Type I, 230V. A universal travel adapter covers it.
