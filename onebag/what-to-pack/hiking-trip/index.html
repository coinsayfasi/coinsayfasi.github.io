What to Pack for a Hiking Trip

A good day hike comes down to footwear, water and the ten essentials — pack light but never skip safety.

Get OneBag — App StoreGoogle Play

Wear & carry

  • Hiking boots/trail shoes
  • Moisture-wicking shirt
  • Rain shell
  • Daypack (15–25L)
  • Trekking poles

Ten essentials

  • Map / GPS
  • Headlamp
  • Sun protection
  • First-aid kit
  • Knife / multi-tool

Fuel & water

  • 2L water / filter
  • High-energy snacks
  • Electrolytes
  • Reusable container
💡 Break in new boots before a long hike to avoid blisters.
💡 Carry more water than you think you need, and treat any refilled water from streams.

Frequently asked questions

What should I bring on a day hike?

Water, snacks, sun protection, a rain layer, a first-aid kit and navigation — the classic ten essentials scaled to the trail.

How much water for a day of hiking?

Plan for about 0.5L per hour, more in heat — carry a filter if refill points exist on the trail.
