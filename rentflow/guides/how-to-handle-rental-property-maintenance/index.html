How to Handle Rental Property Maintenance

Good maintenance keeps tenants happy, protects your asset and avoids costly emergencies. Here's how to handle rental property maintenance the smart way.

Get RentFlow — free

Be proactive

  • Schedule seasonal checks
  • Service heating, cooling and detectors
  • Catch small issues before they grow

Respond to requests fast

  • Acknowledge requests quickly
  • Prioritize safety and habitability
  • Track each request to completion

Keep records and budget

  • Log every repair with cost and date
  • Build a maintenance reserve
  • Track expenses for taxes
⚖️ Before you act: Landlord–tenant rules differ by country and state (notice periods, deposit limits, late-fee caps, eviction steps). Treat this as general best practice and confirm your local law — or ask a local attorney — before applying it.

FAQ

How much should I budget for maintenance?

A common rule of thumb is to set aside ~1% of the property value per year, but it varies. Tracking actual costs gives the real picture.

How do I track maintenance?

Log requests and costs per property. RentFlow handles tenant maintenance requests and stores the history and expenses.
