How to Handle a Difficult Tenant

Most tenant issues can be resolved with calm, consistent, documented action. Here's how to handle a difficult tenant while protecting yourself.

Get RentFlow — free

Stay professional

  • Keep communication calm and in writing
  • Stick to the lease terms
  • Never let it get personal

Document everything

  • Log every incident with dates
  • Keep copies of all communication
  • Record payments and notices

Follow the right process

  • Apply your lease and local law consistently
  • Use proper notices — never shortcuts
  • Seek legal advice for serious cases
⚖️ Before you act: Landlord–tenant rules differ by country and state (notice periods, deposit limits, late-fee caps, eviction steps). Treat this as general best practice and confirm your local law — or ask a local attorney — before applying it.

FAQ

What's the most important thing with a difficult tenant?

Document everything and follow your lease and local law consistently. Clear records protect you if matters escalate.

How do I keep records of issues?

Log incidents, payments and messages with dates. RentFlow keeps a full per-tenant history automatically.
